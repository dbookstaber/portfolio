Selected Work

David Bookstaber began in quantitative finance as a Morgan Stanley intern in 1992. From 2005 to 2007 he worked on the multi-strategy trading system at FrontPoint Partners, which Morgan Stanley acquired. Since 2017 he has consulted through Boniface LLC.

Closed-End Fund Models

Through Boniface LLC he built alpha models of the discounts and premiums at which closed-end funds trade relative to net asset value. Those models became the basis of an investment-management partnership.

Futures Modeling at AQR

As a consultant to AQR Capital Management he worked on short-term expected-return and market-impact models for futures. He is an acknowledged contributor to AQR's April 2014 white paper, Property Catastrophes and Equity Markets, a study of reinsurance risk and equity-market dependencies.

Real-Time Monitors at Magnetar

He was a quantitative analyst at Magnetar Capital, where he built real-time performance and risk monitors for a high-frequency stock trading group, as well as an R&D system for low-frequency strategies.
